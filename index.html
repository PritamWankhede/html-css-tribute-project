Padma Bhushan Ratan N Tata

1937 - 2024

An Inspiring Icon of Business Excellence and Social Impact

Early Years and Education:

1937: Born in Mumbai, India, to Naval Tata and Sooni Tata. 1955: Completed his education at Cornell University in the U.S., with a degree in architecture and structural engineering.

Career at Tata Group:

1962: Joined the Tata Group after working for a brief time in the United States and Europe. 1971: Became the Director of Tata Industries. 1991: Became the Chairman of Tata Group, succeeding J.R.D. Tata. Under his leadership, Tata Group expanded globally and diversified its portfolio.

Key Milestones during His Leadership:

1990s:
1992: Oversaw the acquisition of Tetley Tea by Tata Tea, marking a significant international expansion for Tata Group. 1999: Tata Motors, under his leadership, acquired Daewoo Commercial Vehicle Company from South Korea. 2000s:
2000: Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) became an industry leader in IT outsourcing, helping propel Tata Group into the global IT sector. 2004: Oversaw the acquisition of Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) from Ford, which helped diversify the Group’s automotive portfolio. 2007: Launched the Tata Nano, the world’s cheapest car, which was a significant innovation in the automotive industry. 2008: Under Tata’s leadership, the group bought Corus Group, a leading steel company, expanding Tata Steel’s global footprint. 2010s:
2012: Tata Group’s Tata Global Beverages (formerly Tata Tea) completed the acquisition of Good Earth. 2012: Tata Steel became the sixth-largest steel manufacturer in the world, after the acquisition of Corus. 2012-2013: Tata Group’s Tata Motors led the Indian automotive market with expanded operations in India and the UK.

Post-Retirement Contributions:

2012: Retired from the chairmanship of the Tata Group but remained an influential figure in India’s business landscape. 2014: Focused on philanthropic activities through the Tata Trusts, supporting causes like healthcare, education, and rural development. 2016: Tata Group faced a leadership crisis after Ratan Tata was called back as interim chairman to stabilize the company, a testament to his influence and respect within the organization. 2020-Present: Continues to guide various Tata companies and charities through advisory roles, including enhancing India’s technological infrastructure and contributing to social causes.
